01
विलयन
Concentration | Raoult's Law | Colligative Properties | Osmosis
▼
Solution = Homogeneous mixture of two or more components। Solvent (अधिक मात्रा) + Solute (कम मात्रा)। 9 types of solutions possible (solid, liquid, gas में)।
📊 सांद्रता की इकाइयां | Concentration Units
  • Mass %: (mass of solute / mass of solution) × 100
  • Mole fraction (χ): χA = nA / (nA + nB). Sum = 1
  • Molarity (M): Moles of solute per litre of solution। Temperature dependent
  • Molality (m): Moles of solute per kg of solvent। Temperature independent ✓
  • Normality (N): Gram equivalents per litre
  • ppm: Parts per million — very dilute solutions के लिए
💨 Henry's Law & Raoult's Law
  • Henry's Law: p = KH × χ (Gas की solubility pressure के साथ बढ़ती है)
  • Soft drinks, Scuba diving, Bends (N2 bubbles in blood) → Henry's law
  • Raoult's Law: p = p° × χsolvent (Volatile solute के लिए: p = p°A·χA + p°B·χB)
  • Ideal solution: Obeys Raoult's law। ΔHmix = 0, ΔVmix = 0 (A-A = A-B interaction)
  • +ve deviation: A-B < A-A, B-B interactions। Ethanol + Water। Bp < ideal
  • -ve deviation: A-B > A-A, B-B interactions। Chloroform + Acetone। Bp > ideal
  • Azeotropes: Constant boiling mixtures। Min bp azeotrope (+ve dev) | Max bp (-ve dev)
🌡️ अणुसंख्यक गुण | Colligative Properties
  • Depend only on NUMBER of solute particles — not on nature
  • Relative lowering of VP: Δp/p° = χsolute
  • Elevation of BP (ΔTb): ΔTb = Kb × m (Kb = ebullioscopic constant)
  • Depression of FP (ΔTf): ΔTf = Kf × m (Kf = cryoscopic constant)
  • Water: Kb = 0.52 K kg/mol | Kf = 1.86 K kg/mol
  • Osmosis: Solvent moves from low conc → high conc through semipermeable membrane
  • Osmotic pressure (π): π = CRT = (n/V)RT (van't Hoff equation)
  • Reverse osmosis: External pressure > osmotic pressure। Water purification
  • Isotonic solutions: Same osmotic pressure। 0.9% NaCl = blood plasma
🔢 van't Hoff Factor (i)
  • i = observed colligative property / normal colligative property
  • Electrolytes (dissociate): i > 1 (NaCl → i = 2, CaCl₂ → i = 3)
  • Association (like acetic acid in benzene): i < 1
  • Non-electrolyte: i = 1
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Molality = Temperature independent (PNST mein sabse preferred)
  • Osmotic pressure = π = CRT (van't Hoff)
  • 0.9% NaCl = Normal saline (isotonic with blood)
  • Kf water = 1.86 | Kb water = 0.52 K kg/mol
  • Henry's law — gas solubility ∝ partial pressure
  • i > 1 → dissociation | i < 1 → association

वैद्युत रसायन
Galvanic Cell | EMF | Nernst | Conductance | Electrolysis | Faraday
▼
Electrochemistry = Chemical energy ↔ Electrical energy। Galvanic cell (chemical → electrical) | Electrolytic cell (electrical → chemical)।
🔋 Galvanic Cell | Daniel Cell
  • Zn | ZnSO₄ || CuSO₄ | Cu (Daniel Cell)
  • Anode (-): Oxidation होती है (Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻)
  • Cathode (+): Reduction होती है (Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu)
  • Salt bridge: KCl/KNO₃ — electrical neutrality maintain करता है
  • E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode (Standard electrode potentials)
  • ΔG° = -nFE°cell | ΔG° = -RT ln K
⚡ Nernst Equation
Nernst Equation:
E = E° - (RT/nF) ln Q = E° - (0.0591/n) log Q (at 25°C)
  • Concentration बदलने पर EMF calculate करने के लिए
  • At equilibrium: E = 0, Q = K → E° = (0.0591/n) log K
  • Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE): E° = 0.00 V (reference electrode)
🔬 Conductance | चालकता
  • Resistance (R): R = ρ × l/A (ρ = resistivity)
  • Conductance (G): G = 1/R (unit = Siemens, S)
  • Conductivity (κ): κ = 1/ρ (unit = S cm⁻¹)
  • Molar conductivity (Λm): Λm = κ × 1000/M (S cm² mol⁻¹)
  • Strong electrolyte: Λm increases as dilution increases (Debye-Hückel-Onsager)
  • Weak electrolyte: Sharp increase at high dilution
  • Kohlrausch Law: Λ°m = ν+λ°+ + ν-λ°- (independent migration of ions)
⚡ Electrolysis | Faraday's Laws
  • First law: Mass deposited ∝ quantity of electricity (W = ZQ = ZIt)
  • Second law: Same Q → masses deposited ∝ equivalent weights
  • 1 Faraday = 96500 C (charge of 1 mole of electrons)
  • Electrolysis of water: H₂ at cathode, O₂ at anode
  • Electrolysis of NaCl solution: H₂ + Cl₂ + NaOH (chlor-alkali process)
🔋 Batteries
  • Primary: Non-rechargeable। Dry cell (Leclanche) = Zn-MnO₂
  • Secondary: Rechargeable। Lead storage battery (12V car battery)
  • Fuel cell: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O + Electricity। 70% efficiency। No pollution
  • Corrosion: Fe → Fe²⁺ (anode). O₂ + H₂O → OH⁻ (cathode). Prevention: Galvanization (Zn coating), Cathodic protection
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Anode = Oxidation (-) | Cathode = Reduction (+)
  • 1 Faraday = 96500 C = charge of 1 mole electrons
  • E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode
  • ΔG° = -nFE°cell
  • SHE = Standard reference (E° = 0.00 V)
  • Kohlrausch Law → molar conductivity at infinite dilution

03
रासायनिक बलगतिकी
Rate of Reaction | Order | Half-life | Arrhenius | Collision Theory
▼
Chemical Kinetics = Rate of chemical reactions और उन्हें affect करने वाले factors का अध्ययन।
📈 अभिक्रिया दर | Rate of Reaction
  • Average rate: Δ[concentration] / Δtime
  • Instantaneous rate: -d[R]/dt = d[P]/dt (tangent to concentration-time graph)
  • Rate = k[A]ˣ[B]ʸ (k = rate constant, x+y = overall order)
  • Factors: Concentration, Temperature, Catalyst, Surface area, Light
📊 अभिक्रिया की कोटि | Order of Reaction
  • Zero order: Rate = k। t½ = [R]₀/2k। Example: Decomposition of NH₃ on Pt surface
  • First order: Rate = k[A]। t½ = 0.693/k (independent of concentration!)। ln[R]t = ln[R]₀ - kt
  • Example: Radioactive decay, Hydrolysis of sucrose in acidic solution
  • Second order: Rate = k[A]²। t½ = 1/k[A]₀
  • Pseudo-first order: Second order reaction → behaves as first order (excess one reactant)
  • Example: Acid hydrolysis of ester (water in excess)
🌡️ Arrhenius Equation
Arrhenius Equation:
k = A·e^(-Ea/RT) → ln k = ln A - Ea/RT
  • Ea = Activation energy — minimum energy required for reaction
  • A = Pre-exponential factor / Frequency factor
  • Temperature ↑ → k increases exponentially
  • 10°C rise → reaction rate doubles (approximately)
  • Catalyst → Ea कम करता है → reaction faster
💥 Collision Theory
  • Reaction = effective collisions (sufficient energy + proper orientation)
  • Rate = PZABe^(-Ea/RT) (P = steric factor, Z = collision frequency)
  • Transition state theory: Activated complex at energy maximum
  • Molecularity = number of molecules in elementary step (1, 2, or 3)
  • Order ≠ Molecularity (Order is experimental, Molecularity is theoretical)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • t½ for first order = 0.693/k (concentration independent)
  • Radioactive decay = First order reaction
  • Pseudo-first order: ester + H₂O (H₂O excess)
  • Arrhenius: k = Ae^(-Ea/RT)
  • Catalyst → Ea कम करता है, ΔG change नहीं
  • Order = experimentally determined, Molecularity = theoretical

d एवं f ब्लॉक के तत्व
Transition Metals | Lanthanoids | Actinoids | KMnO₄ | K₂Cr₂O₇
▼
d-block = Groups 3-12 (Transition metals)। f-block = Lanthanoids (58-71) + Actinoids (90-103)। (n-1)d orbitals fill होते हैं।
🔬 d-Block की सामान्य विशेषताएं | General Properties
  • Electronic config: (n-1)d¹⁻¹⁰ ns⁰⁻²। Exception: Cr=[Ar]3d⁵4s¹, Cu=[Ar]3d¹⁰4s¹
  • Variable oxidation states: Multiple OS because d-electrons involved। Mn: +2 to +7
  • Coloured compounds: d-d transitions → absorb visible light
  • Magnetic properties: Unpaired d electrons → Paramagnetic. Cu²⁺ more paramagnetic than Cu⁺
  • Catalytic activity: Variable OS + ability to form complexes → good catalysts
  • Interstitial compounds: Small atoms (H, C, N) fit in interstitial voids of metal lattice
  • Alloy formation: Similar atomic radii → substitute each other
  • Atomic radii: decreases across period, then roughly constant (lanthanide contraction in 5d)
  • Ionisation enthalpy: higher than s-block, lower than p-block
🔴 KMnO₄ — Potassium Permanganate
  • Preparation: MnO₂ + KOH + O₂ → K₂MnO₄ → (acidify) → KMnO₄
  • Deep purple/violet colored crystals। Strong oxidising agent
  • Acidic medium: MnO₄⁻ → Mn²⁺ (colorless), gain 5e⁻
  • Neutral/faintly basic: MnO₄⁻ → MnO₂ (brown ppt), gain 3e⁻
  • Strongly basic: MnO₄⁻ → MnO₄²⁻ (manganate), gain 1e⁻
  • Uses: Antiseptic (Dettol alternative), Titration (no indicator needed)
🟡 K₂Cr₂O₇ — Potassium Dichromate
  • Orange colored crystals। Cr in +6 oxidation state
  • Preparation: Chrome iron ore → Na₂CrO₄ → K₂Cr₂O₇
  • Cr₂O₇²⁻ + H₂O ⇌ 2CrO₄²⁻ + 2H⁺ (acidic = dichromate orange, basic = chromate yellow)
  • Strong oxidising agent in acidic medium: Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6e⁻ → 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O
  • Uses: Leather tanning, Textile dyeing, Chrome alum preparation
🌿 f-Block Elements
  • Lanthanoids (La-Lu, 57-71): 4f filling। Ce to Lu
  • Lanthanide contraction: Poor shielding of 4f electrons → size decreases across series
  • Lanthanoids mostly show +3 OS। Ce: +4 (stable), Eu: +2
  • Actinoids (Ac-Lr, 89-103): 5f filling। Radioactive। U, Pu, Th — nuclear energy
  • Actinoids show more variable OS than lanthanoids (5f more accessible)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Cr = [Ar]3d⁵4s¹, Cu = [Ar]3d¹⁰4s¹ (exceptions)
  • KMnO₄: Acidic → Mn²⁺ (5e⁻) | Basic → MnO₂ (3e⁻)
  • Mn shows maximum oxidation states (+2 to +7)
  • Lanthanide contraction → 5d series similar size to 4d
  • Coloured compounds: d-d transition

05
उपसहसंयोजन यौगिक
Werner's Theory | Ligands | IUPAC | Isomerism | Crystal Field Theory
▼
Coordination compounds = Central metal atom/ion + Ligands। Alfred Werner (1893) ने theory दी। Nobel Prize 1913।
📋 Werner's Theory | Terminology
  • Central atom: Metal ion जो ligands accept करता है
  • Ligand: Electron pair donor (Lewis base)। Monodentate (1 donor), Bidentate (2), Polydentate
  • Coordination number: Ligand donor atoms की संख्या central metal के around
  • Coordination sphere: Square bracket [ ] में — central metal + ligands
  • Oxidation number: Central metal का charge (ligand charges से calculate)
  • Example: [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺ → Co = +3, NH₃ = neutral, CN = 6
🔗 Ligands के प्रकार | Types of Ligands
  • Monodentate: Cl⁻, NH₃, H₂O, CN⁻, NO₂⁻, SCN⁻
  • Bidentate: en (ethylenediamine), ox²⁻ (oxalate), acac (acetylacetonate)
  • Polydentate/Chelating: EDTA (hexadentate) — 6 donor atoms
  • Ambidentate: Two different donor atoms — NO₂⁻ (N or O), SCN⁻ (S or N)
  • Chelate effect: Polydentate > Monodentate in stability (entropy reason)
📝 IUPAC Nomenclature
  • Order: Ligands alphabetically, then metal, then oxidation state in Roman numerals
  • Cation first, then anion
  • Anionic ligands: -o suffix (Cl⁻=chloro, CN⁻=cyano, NO₂⁻=nitro/nitrito)
  • Neutral ligands: aqua (H₂O), ammine (NH₃), carbonyl (CO), nitrosyl (NO)
  • Example: [Co(NH₃)₄Cl₂]Cl = Tetraamminedichloridocobalt(III) chloride
🔄 Isomerism
  • Structural isomerism: Linkage (NO₂/ONO), Ionisation, Solvate, Coordination
  • Stereo isomerism: Geometrical (cis-trans) + Optical (mirror image)
  • cis-[Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂] = Cisplatin (anti-cancer drug)
  • Square planar: cis-trans possible | Octahedral: cis-trans + optical
💎 Crystal Field Theory (CFT)
  • Ligands = point charges. d-orbitals split in presence of ligands
  • Octahedral: d splits into eg (higher) + t₂g (lower). Δo = crystal field splitting energy
  • Strong field ligands (CO, CN⁻): large Δo → low spin | Weak field (Cl⁻, F⁻): small Δo → high spin
  • Spectrochemical series: I⁻ < Br⁻ < Cl⁻ < F⁻ < OH⁻ < en < CN⁻ < CO
  • Colour = complementary color of absorbed light (d-d transition)
🌟 Important Applications
  • Cisplatin [Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂] — Anticancer drug
  • Haemoglobin — Fe²⁺ coordination compound (O₂ transport)
  • Chlorophyll — Mg²⁺ coordination compound (photosynthesis)
  • Vitamin B₁₂ (Cyanocobalamin) — Co³⁺ coordination compound
  • EDTA — Metal poisoning treatment
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Cisplatin = cis-[Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂] = Anticancer drug
  • EDTA = Hexadentate ligand (6 donor atoms)
  • Haemoglobin = Fe²⁺ complex, Chlorophyll = Mg²⁺ complex
  • Ambidentate ligands: SCN⁻, NO₂⁻
  • Strong field ligands → low spin (CO, CN⁻)

हैलोऐल्केन तथा हैलोऐरीन
SN1 | SN2 | DDT | Freons | Optical Isomerism
▼
Haloalkanes = sp³ C-X bond (alkyl halide)। Haloarenes = sp² C-X bond (aryl halide)। C-X bond polar (δ+C - δ-X) due to electronegativity।
🧪 Preparation Methods
  • From alcohols: R-OH + HX → R-X + H₂O (HI > HBr > HCl reactivity)
  • Halogenation: Alkane + X₂ (UV light) → Alkyl halide (free radical)
  • Sandmeyer reaction: ArN₂⁺ + CuX → ArX (prepare aryl halides)
  • Finkelstein: R-Cl + NaI (acetone) → R-I + NaCl (exchange reaction)
  • Swarts reaction: R-Cl + AgF → R-F (prepare alkyl fluorides)
⚡ Nucleophilic Substitution
  • SN2 (Bimolecular): One step, Backside attack, Inversion of configuration (Walden inversion). Favoured by: Primary alkyl halides, Polar aprotic solvents
  • SN1 (Unimolecular): Two steps, Carbocation intermediate, Racemisation. Favoured by: Tertiary alkyl halides, Polar protic solvents
  • Reactivity order for SN1: 3° > 2° > 1° > Methyl
  • Reactivity order for SN2: Methyl > 1° > 2° > 3°
🔄 Elimination vs Substitution
  • E2 (bimolecular elimination): Strong base, high temperature → Alkene (Saytzeff rule)
  • Saytzeff rule: More substituted alkene (stable) preferentially formed
  • Tertiary + strong base → E2 preferred over SN2
🌿 Polyhalogen Compounds
  • Chloroform (CHCl₃): Anaesthetic। Oxidised to COCl₂ (phosgene) — toxic। Store in dark bottles
  • Carbon tetrachloride (CCl₄): Fire extinguisher (Pyrene). Non-polar solvent
  • DDT: First organic insecticide। Paul Müller — Nobel 1948। Non-biodegradable → biomagnification
  • Freons (CFCs): Refrigerants। Ozone depletion। Montreal Protocol (1987) — banned
  • Iodoform (CHI₃): Antiseptic। Yellow solid. Iodoform test for CH₃CO- groups
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • SN2 = Inversion (Walden inversion), one step, primary favoured
  • SN1 = Racemisation, two steps, tertiary favoured
  • DDT = Non-biodegradable, biomagnification
  • Freons = CFC → Ozone depletion
  • Iodoform test = CH₃CO- group detection
  • Sandmeyer = Aryl halide from diazonium salt

Unimolecular, two steps। Carbocation intermediate। Racemisation। Tertiary alkyl halide + polar protic solvent।
DDT ★ PNST
Dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane। First chlorinated insecticide। Paul Müller Nobel 1948। Non-biodegradable → biomagnification → banned।
Freons (CFCs)
Chlorofluorocarbons। Refrigerants, Propellants। CFC → Stratosphere → Cl radical → Ozone destruction। Montreal Protocol 1987।
Sandmeyer Reaction
ArN₂⁺Cl⁻ + CuCl → ArCl। Diazonium salt से aryl halide। Cu catalyst essential।

ऐल्कोहॉल, फीनॉल एवं ईथर
Lucas Test | Reimer-Tiemann | Victor-Meyer | Williamson
▼
Alcohols = R-OH। Phenols = Ar-OH। Ethers = R-O-R'। O-H bond polar → H-bonding → high BP।
🍺 Alcohols — Preparation & Properties
  • From alkenes: Acid-catalysed hydration (Markovnikov's rule)
  • From Grignard reagent: RMgX + HCHO → 1° alcohol; RMgX + RCHO → 2°; RMgX + R₂CO → 3°
  • Acidity order: H₂O > 1° > 2° > 3° (but phenol >> alcohols)
  • Lucas test: Distinguish 1°, 2°, 3° alcohols with conc.HCl + ZnCl₂
  • 3° = turbidity immediately | 2° = turbidity in 5 min | 1° = no turbidity (at room temp)
  • Oxidation: 1° → Aldehyde → Carboxylic acid | 2° → Ketone | 3° = Resistant
  • Dehydration: Conc. H₂SO₄, 443K → Alkene (E2) | 413K → Ether
  • Victor Meyer test: Distinguish 1°, 2°, 3° using P/I₂ + AgNO₂ + HNO₂
🧪 Phenols — Special Reactions
  • Phenol more acidic than alcohols (phenoxide ion resonance stabilized)
  • Reimer-Tiemann reaction: Phenol + CHCl₃ + NaOH → ortho-hydroxybenzaldehyde (Salicylaldehyde)
  • Kolbe's reaction: Sodium phenoxide + CO₂ (300K, 5 atm) → Salicylic acid (Aspirin precursor)
  • Diazo coupling: Phenol + ArN₂⁺ → Azo dye (orange-red color)
  • Phenol + Br₂ water → 2,4,6-tribromophenol (white ppt) — test for phenol
  • Phenol + FeCl₃ → Violet color (test for phenol)
🌫️ Ethers
  • Williamson synthesis: R-ONa + R'-X → R-O-R' + NaX (best method)
  • Diethyl ether (C₂H₅OC₂H₅) = Common anaesthetic
  • Ethers — poor nucleophiles, low reactivity। Cleavage with HI/HBr at high temp
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Lucas test: 3°=immediate turbidity, 1°=no turbidity
  • Reimer-Tiemann = Phenol → Salicylaldehyde
  • Kolbe's = Sodium phenoxide → Salicylic acid (Aspirin)
  • Phenol + FeCl₃ = Violet color (test)
  • Phenol + Br₂ water = White ppt (2,4,6-tribromophenol)
  • Williamson synthesis = Best method for ethers

▼
Aldehydes (R-CHO) और Ketones (R-CO-R') में C=O (carbonyl) group होता है। Nucleophilic addition reactions characteristic हैं।
🔬 Preparation
  • Aldehydes from 1° alcohols: PCC (Pyridinium chlorochromate) — mild oxidation
  • Rosenmund reduction: Acid chloride + H₂/Pd-BaSO₄ → Aldehyde
  • Stephen reaction: Nitrile + SnCl₂/HCl → Aldehyde (after hydrolysis)
  • Ketones from 2° alcohols: K₂Cr₂O₇/H₂SO₄ oxidation
  • Ketones from carboxylic acids: Dry distillation of Ca salt
  • Ozonolysis: Alkene + O₃ → Aldehyde/Ketone

  • Decarboxylation: RCOOH + NaOH (CaO) → R-H (soda lime)
  • Fischer esterification: RCOOH + R'OH ⇌ RCOOR' + H₂O (acid catalyst)

  • Aldol condensation = α-H required
  • Cannizzaro = No α-H (HCHO, C₆H₅CHO)
  • HVZ reaction = α-bromination of acids

ऐमीन
Gabriel | Hoffmann | Diazonium Salts | Basicity | Coupling
▼
Amines = NH₃ से derived compounds। N पर lone pair → Basic character। Primary (1°), Secondary (2°), Tertiary (3°) amines।
🔬 Preparation Methods
  • Reduction of nitro compounds: ArNO₂ + Fe/HCl (or H₂/Ni) → ArNH₂
  • Gabriel synthesis: Phthalimide + KOH + RX → N-alkylphthalimide → (hydrolysis) → Primary amine
  • Only primary amines। 2° and 3° cannot be prepared
  • Hoffmann bromamide degradation: RCONH₂ + Br₂ + NaOH → RNH₂ (one C less)
  • Reductive amination: Aldehyde/Ketone + NH₃ + H₂ → Amine
  • Reduction of nitriles: R-CN + LiAlH₄ → R-CH₂-NH₂
⚖️ Basicity of Amines
  • Aliphatic amines > NH₃ > Aromatic amines (resonance delocalises lone pair in ArNH₂)
  • In gas phase: 3° > 2° > 1° > NH₃ (inductive effect)
  • In aqueous solution: 2° > 1° > 3° > NH₃ (steric + hydration effects)
  • Electron donating groups (CH₃) increase basicity
  • Electron withdrawing groups (NO₂, Cl) decrease basicity
  • Aniline < p-nitroaniline (NO₂ withdraws e⁻ → less basic)
🧪 Chemical Reactions
  • Carbylamine test (Isocyanide test): 1° amine + CHCl₃ + KOH → Isocyanide (bad smell)
  • Only primary amines give this test
  • Hinsberg test: Benzenesulfonyl chloride — distinguish 1°, 2°, 3° amines
  • Coupling reaction: ArNH₂ + HNO₂ (0-5°C) → Diazonium salt → Azo dye
🔴 Diazonium Salts (ArN₂⁺X⁻)
  • Preparation: ArNH₂ + NaNO₂ + HCl (0-5°C) → ArN₂⁺Cl⁻ (Diazotization)
  • Unstable (kept at 0-5°C). Used as intermediates in organic synthesis
  • Sandmeyer reaction: ArN₂⁺ + CuX → ArX (X = Cl, Br, CN)
  • Gattermann reaction: ArN₂⁺ + Cu/HCl → ArCl (without Cu salt)
  • ArN₂⁺ + H₂O → ArOH (phenol) | ArN₂⁺ + HBF₄ → ArF (Balz-Schiemann)
  • Coupling: ArN₂⁺ + Phenol/Aniline → Azo compounds (dyes)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Carbylamine test = 1° amine only (bad smell isocyanide)
  • Gabriel synthesis = Only primary amines
  • Hoffmann bromamide = Product has one less C
  • Basicity: Aliphatic > NH₃ > Aromatic amines
  • Diazonium salts at 0-5°C (unstable)
  • Sandmeyer = CuX needed | Gattermann = Cu metal + HX

10
जैव अणु
Carbohydrates | Proteins | Enzymes | Vitamins | Nucleic Acids | Hormones
▼
Biomolecules = जीवित systems के chemical compounds। Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids, Nucleic acids, Vitamins — सब essential।
🍬 कार्बोहाइड्रेट | Carbohydrates
  • Polyhydroxy aldehydes/ketones या compounds जो hydrolysis पर ये देते हैं
  • Monosaccharides: Cannot hydrolyse further। Glucose (Aldose), Fructose (Ketose)
  • Oligosaccharides: 2-10 monosaccharides। Sucrose (Glucose + Fructose), Maltose (Glucose + Glucose), Lactose (Glucose + Galactose)
  • Polysaccharides: Starch (amylose + amylopectin), Cellulose, Glycogen (animal starch)
  • Reducing sugars: Free aldehyde/ketone group। Glucose, Fructose, Maltose, Lactose। React with Tollens/Fehling
  • Non-reducing: Sucrose (no free OH) — glycosidic bond between C1 of glucose and C2 of fructose
  • Glucose: CHO-CHOH×4-CH₂OH। α-D-Glucose (1,4-glycosidic in starch) | β-D-Glucose (1,4-β in cellulose)
  • Cellulose: β-1,4-glycosidic. Non-digestible by humans (no β-glucosidase)
🥚 प्रोटीन | Proteins
  • α-Amino acids के polymers — linked by peptide bonds (-CO-NH-)
  • 20 essential amino acids। Glycine = simplest (no chiral C)
  • Primary structure: Amino acid sequence
  • Secondary structure: α-helix (H-bonds within chain) + β-pleated sheet
  • Tertiary structure: 3D folding — S-S bonds, H-bonds, hydrophobic
  • Quaternary: Multiple polypeptide chains (e.g., Haemoglobin — 4 chains)
  • Denaturation: Secondary/tertiary structure disrupted — biological activity lost
  • Fibrous proteins: Keratin (hair), Myosin (muscle) — structural
  • Globular proteins: Enzymes, Hormones, Antibodies — functional
⚙️ Enzymes
  • Biological catalysts। Protein in nature (except Ribozyme = RNA)
  • Lock and key model (Fischer): Active site exactly fits substrate
  • Induced fit model (Koshland): Active site changes shape to fit substrate
  • Coenzyme = Non-protein organic part। Cofactor = Metal ion
  • Vitamins often form coenzymes
💊 Vitamins
  • Fat soluble: A, D, E, K (ADEK)। Stored in liver/adipose
  • Water soluble: B complex (B1, B2, B6, B12), C। Not stored — daily needed
  • Vit A — Night blindness। B1 (Thiamine) — Beriberi। B12 — Pernicious anaemia
  • Vit C — Scurvy। D — Rickets। K — Blood clotting
🧬 Nucleic Acids
  • Nucleotide = Base + Sugar + Phosphate
  • DNA: Deoxyribose + A, T, G, C। Double stranded helix
  • RNA: Ribose + A, U, G, C। Single stranded
  • A-T (2 H-bonds), G-C (3 H-bonds)
🧪 Hormones
  • Insulin (polypeptide) — Blood glucose regulate। Produced by β cells of islets of Langerhans
  • Adrenaline (amino acid derivative) — Emergency hormone
  • Sex hormones (Testosterone, Estrogen) — Steroid hormones
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Sucrose = Non-reducing sugar (no free aldehyde/ketone)
  • Cellulose = β-1,4-glycosidic bond (non-digestible)
  • Glycogen = Animal starch (liver + muscle)
  • Denaturation = 3D structure destroy → activity loss
  • Vit A = Night blindness | D = Rickets | C = Scurvy | K = Clotting
  • Enzyme = Biological catalyst (protein, except Ribozyme)
