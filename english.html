🔠 Noun के प्रकार | Types of Nouns
  • Proper Noun: किसी विशेष का नाम — Joyce, Dublin, India
  • Common Noun: सामान्य नाम — girl, city, book, doctor
  • Abstract Noun: जिसे छू न सकें — freedom, love, beauty, fear
  • Collective Noun: समूह का नाम — committee, family, team, flock
  • Material Noun: पदार्थ का नाम — water, gold, wood, paper
🔧 Verb के प्रकार | Types of Verbs
  • Transitive: Object होता है — She wrote a story.
  • Intransitive: Object नहीं — Eveline sat. / He smiled.
  • Auxiliary: सहायक — is, was, have, will, do
  • Modal: सम्भावना/इच्छा — can, could, may, might, must, should, would
  • Phrasal Verb: Verb + Preposition — give up, look into, break down
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Noun के 5 types (Proper, Common, Abstract, Collective, Material)
  • Pronoun: Subjective(I,he,she) | Objective(me,him,her) | Possessive(my,his)
  • Modal verbs → Possibility/Necessity: can, may, must, should
  • Preposition: in (enclosed place), on (surface), at (point)
  • Coordinating Conjunctions: FANBOYS (For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So)

Simple | Continuous | Perfect | Perfect Continuous — Past/Present/Future
▼
अंग्रेजी में 12 Tenses होते हैं (3 times × 4 aspects)। Tense = वह समय जब कोई कार्य होता है। PNST में Tense सबसे ज़्यादा पूछा जाता है।
📋 Tense Chart (12 Tenses)
TenseFormulaExample
Simple PresentV₁ / V₁+s/esShe reads every day.
Present Continuousis/am/are + V-ingShe is reading now.
Present Perfecthave/has + V₃She has read the book.
Present Perfect Cont.have/has been + V-ingShe has been reading for 2 hours.
Simple PastV₂ / did + V₁She read the book yesterday.
Past Continuouswas/were + V-ingShe was reading when I came.

⚠️ Important Tense Rules
  • He/She/It + Simple Present: Always add -s/-es → She goes, He writes, It runs
  • Since vs For: Since = point of time (since 2020) | For = period (for 2 hours)
  • Used to: Past habit — She used to walk daily. (अब नहीं करती)
  • Would: Past habit (literary) — He would sit by the window every evening.
  • Stative verbs: No continuous form — know, believe, love, want, see (wrong: I am knowing)
  • Just/Already/Yet: Present Perfect के साथ — I have just eaten. | Have you eaten yet?
  • Conditional: If + Past → would + V₁ → If I had money, I would travel.
जब वह पहुँचा, ट्रेन जा चुकी थी।
By the time he arrived, the train had left. → Past Perfect used for earlier event
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • He/She/It → Simple Present → always add -s/-es
  • Since = point | For = duration (with Present/Past Perfect)
  • Past Perfect: जब दो past events हों → पहले वाले के लिए had + V₃
  • Stative verbs: No -ing form (know, love, want, see, believe)
  • Conditional: If + Simple Past → would + V₁
  • Just, Already, Yet, Ever, Never → Present Perfect

कथन परिवर्तन (Direct & Indirect Speech)
Assertive | Interrogative | Imperative | Tense Backshift | Reporting Verbs
▼
Direct Speech = किसी के exact words (inverted commas में)। Indirect Speech = उन्हीं words को अपने शब्दों में (commas हटाकर)।

📝 Sentence Type Rules
  • Assertive → that: She said, "I am tired." → She said that she was tired.
  • Yes/No Question → if/whether: He asked, "Are you free?" → He asked if she was free.
  • Wh-Question → same Wh-word: She asked, "Where do you live?" → She asked where I lived.
  • Imperative (order) → to/not to: He said, "Sit down." → He told her to sit down.
  • Request → requested to: He said, "Please help me." → He requested her to help him.
  • Exclamatory → exclaimed that: She said, "What a beautiful day!" → She exclaimed that it was a beautiful day.
⚠️ Important: Reporting Verb PRESENT हो तो tense नहीं बदलता!

She says, "I am tired." → She says that she is tired. (No backshift — says = present)

⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Reporting verb Past हो → Tense backshift होता है
  • Reporting verb Present/Future हो → No backshift
  • Universal truths never backshift: She said, "The sun rises in the east." → Said that the sun rises (not rose)
  • Yes/No Q → if/whether | Wh-Q → same Wh word
  • Imperative: told/ordered/requested + to + V₁

04
वाच्य परिवर्तन (Active & Passive Voice)
Subject | Object | By | V₃ | All Tenses Passive Forms
▼
Active Voice = Subject कार्य करता है। Passive Voice = Subject पर कार्य होता है। Object → Subject बन जाता है। Verb = be + V₃।
🔑 Golden Rule for Active → Passive

Step 1: Active का Object → Passive का Subject
Step 2: Active का Subject → "by + Object" (end में)
Step 3: Verb → Appropriate form of "be" + V₃
Step 4: Object Pronoun change: he→him, she→her, they→them, I→me, we→us

📋 All Tense Passive Forms
TenseActivePassive Formula
Simple PresentShe writes a letter.is/am/are + written
Present Cont.She is writing a letter.is/am/are + being + written
Present PerfectShe has written a letter.have/has + been + written
Simple PastShe wrote a letter.was/were + written
Past ContinuousShe was writing.was/were + being + written
Past PerfectShe had written.had + been + written
Simple FutureShe will write.will + be + written
Future PerfectShe will have written.will + have been + written
ModalsShe can write it.can + be + written
⚠️ Special Cases
  • Imperative Passive: "Open the door." → "Let the door be opened."
  • Intransitive verbs: Passive नहीं बनती (no object) — She smiled. / He arrived.
  • "by" हटाना: जब doer unknown/obvious हो — The thief was caught. (by police — obvious)
  • Double object: Both can become subject — He gave her a book. → She was given a book. / A book was given to her.
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Object → Subject | Subject → by + Object pronoun
  • he→him, she→her, they→them, I→me, we→us
  • Intransitive verbs → Passive possible नहीं
  • Imperative → Let + object + be + V₃
  • Modal passive: can/must/should + be + V₃

Passive Voice Formula ★ PNST
Object + be(appropriate) + V₃ + by + Subject। He wrote the book → The book was written by him।
Pronoun Change in Passive ★ PNST
He→him, She→her, They→them, I→me, We→us, Who→whom। Subject case → Object case।

05
Articles (A, An, The)
Definite | Indefinite | Zero Article | Vowel Sound | Unique Nouns
▼
Articles तीन होते हैं: A, An (Indefinite), The (Definite)। A/An = पहली बार mention। The = already known/specific। Some nouns के साथ article नहीं।
📌 A vs An — Vowel Sound Rule
✅ "An" use करो when:
  • Vowel SOUND से शुरू हो
  • an apple, an egg
  • an hour (/aʊər/ = vowel)
  • an MP (/em/ = vowel)
  • an honest man
  • an umbrella
⚠️ "A" use करो when:
  • Consonant SOUND से शुरू हो
  • a book, a table
  • a university (/ju:/ = consonant)
  • a union, a unique
  • a one-rupee coin
  • a European
📌 "The" Use करो when:
  • Already mentioned: I saw a dog. The dog was black.
  • Unique/One of a kind: The sun, the moon, the earth, the sky
  • Superlatives: The best, the tallest, the most beautiful
  • Rivers, Seas, Oceans: The Ganga, The Pacific, The Nile, The Bay of Bengal
  • Mountain ranges: The Himalayas, The Alps (but: Mount Everest — no the)
  • Countries (plural/union): The USA, The UK, The UAE
  • Newspapers: The Hindu, The Times of India
  • Musical instruments: She plays the guitar, He plays the sitar
  • Ordinal numbers: The first, the second, the last
🚫 No Article (Zero Article) when:
  • Proper nouns: India, Delhi, Joyce, Shakespeare (generally)
  • Languages: She speaks Hindi. (not "the Hindi")
  • Meals: I had breakfast/lunch/dinner.
  • Uncountable nouns: Water is essential. Gold is precious.
  • Plural common nouns (general): Books are important. Dogs bark.
  • Diseases (usually): She has fever/malaria. (but: the flu)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • An = vowel SOUND (not letter): an hour, an honest, an MP
  • A = consonant SOUND: a university, a union, a European
  • The = rivers, seas, mountain ranges, newspapers, unique nouns
  • No article: Languages, meals, proper nouns, uncountable (general)
  • Musical instruments → always "the": plays the piano

06
Error Correction (त्रुटि सुधार)
Subject-Verb Agreement | Pronoun | Preposition | Article | Common Errors
▼
PNST में Error Correction बहुत पूछी जाती है। सबसे common errors: Subject-Verb Agreement, Wrong tense, Preposition, Article errors।
⚖️ Subject-Verb Agreement Rules
  • "A number of" → Plural verb: A number of students ARE present. (not is)
  • "The number of" → Singular verb: The number of students IS increasing.
  • Each/Every/Either/Neither → Singular: Each of them HAS a book.
  • None → Singular (strict) / Plural (informal): None of them IS ready.
  • Collective nouns → Singular: The team IS playing well.
  • "As well as" → First subject decides: She as well as her friends IS happy.
  • "Either...or / Neither...nor" → Nearest subject: Neither he nor I AM wrong.
🔠 Common Word Confusions
Wrong ❌Right ✅Rule
Its very hot.It's very hot.Its=possession | It's=it is
There bags are here.Their bags are here.Their=possession
She is more taller.She is taller.No "more" with -er
He is elder than me.He is older than me.Elder = family only
I met him yesterday night.I met him last night.Last night, not yesterday night
She returned back home.She returned home.Return = already means back
He is my cousin brother.He is my cousin.Cousin itself = cousin
I am having a pen.I have a pen.Have (possession) = stative
She is knowing the answer.She knows the answer.Know = stative verb
He did not went.He did not go.Did + V₁ (base form)
🔗 Preposition Errors
  • Married to (not with): She is married to him.
  • Interested in: I am interested in reading.
  • Good at: She is good at maths.
  • Angry with (person) / at (thing): Angry with him / angry at the situation.
  • Die of (disease): He died of cancer. (not from)
  • Arrive at (small place) / in (large place): at station, in Mumbai.
  • Discuss (no preposition): We discussed the matter. (not discussed about)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • "A number of" → Plural | "The number of" → Singular
  • Did/Does/Do + V₁ (base form) — never V₂
  • More + comparative = Wrong (more taller ❌)
  • Stative verbs: No -ing (knowing ❌, having for possession ❌)
  • Its = possession | It's = it is | Their ≠ There ≠ They're

07
गद्यांश पठन (Reading Comprehension)
Skimming | Scanning | Inference | Vocabulary in Context | Tone
▼
Reading Comprehension = Passage पढ़कर questions का answer देना। PNST में 4-5 questions passage से पूछे जाते हैं।
📖 Reading Strategies
  • Skimming: Quickly पढ़ना main idea के लिए — title, headings, first sentences पर focus
  • Scanning: Specific information ढूंढना — names, dates, numbers, keywords
  • Inference: Directly न कहा हो तो context से समझना — "Read between the lines"
  • Vocabulary in context: Passage के आसपास के words से meaning निकालना
  • Tone/Attitude: Author का attitude — Critical, Sarcastic, Neutral, Appreciative
❓ Question Types & How to Answer
Question TypeStrategyExample
Factual (Where/Who/When)Direct answer from passageWhere was Eveline sitting?
InferentialConclusion से निकालोWhat does 'invade' suggest?
VocabularyContext clues use करोWhat does 'melancholy' mean here?
Title/ThemeMain idea summarize करोSuitable title for the passage?
Antonym/SynonymOpposite/similar meaningOpposite of 'alienation'?
✍️ Answer Writing Tips
  • Answer Complete sentence में दो, না सिर्फ words में
  • Passage के words use कर सकते हो — अपने words में भी OK
  • Answer passage के order में आता है (usually)
  • "According to the passage" phrase use करो factual answers में
  • Inference questions में reasoning दो — "This suggests that..."
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Skimming = Main idea | Scanning = Specific info
  • Inferential = Not directly stated, conclusion निकालो
  • Always answer in complete sentences
  • Vocabulary: context से meaning निकालो, dictionary rely मत करो
  • Tone words: Critical, Sarcastic, Ironic, Appreciative, Neutral, Melancholic

Literary & Poetic Devices
Simile | Metaphor | Personification | Alliteration | Imagery | Symbolism
▼
Literary devices = लेखक अपनी बात को प्रभावशाली ढंग से कहने के लिए use करता है। इन्हें पहचानना और उनका effect बताना जरूरी है।

🔍 Simile vs Metaphor — EASY TRICK
Simile
  • like या as होता है
  • He is like a lion.
  • She runs as fast as a deer.
  • Comparison = indirect
Metaphor
  • like/as नहीं होता
  • He is a lion.
  • Life is a journey.
  • Comparison = direct
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Simile = like/as | Metaphor = direct comparison
  • Personification = non-living gets human qualities
  • Alliteration = same initial consonant (not vowel)

वाक्य संरचना (Sentence Structure)
Simple | Compound | Complex | Clauses | Conjunctions
▼
Sentence Structure grammar का important part है। Simple, Compound, Complex sentences को identify करना और बनाना आना चाहिए।
📐 Types of Sentences
  • Simple Sentence: एक Subject + एक Predicate (1 independent clause) — She was tired.
  • Compound Sentence: 2 Independent clauses + Coordinating conjunction (FANBOYS) — She was tired, but she continued.
  • Complex Sentence: 1 Main clause + 1 Subordinate clause — Although she was tired, she continued.
  • Compound-Complex: Compound + Complex combined — She sat and watched, although she was tired.
🔗 Types of Clauses
  • Noun Clause: Sentence में noun का काम करे — That she was tired is clear. (Subject)
  • Adjective Clause: Noun को modify करे (who, which, that) — The book that I read was excellent.
  • Adverb Clause: Verb को modify करे — She left before he arrived.

⭐ PNST Important Points
  • FANBOYS = For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So (Coordinating conjunctions)
  • Although/Though = Contrast (two clauses present)
  • Because/Since/As = Reason

  • Adjective clause = who, which, that (relative pronouns)

Although vs Despite

🔄 Transformation Rules
  • Positive → Comparative: No other + Comparative + than — She is taller than any other girl in class.
  • Comparative → Superlative: The + Superlative — She is the tallest girl in class.
  • Superlative → Positive: No other... as + Positive + as — No other girl is as tall as she.
Sachin is the greatest cricketer. (Superlative)
= No other cricketer is as great as Sachin. (Positive)
= Sachin is greater than any other cricketer. (Comparative)
⚠️ Common Errors in Comparison
  • "More taller" / "Most tallest" ❌ — Never use "more/most" with -er/-est forms
  • "Elder" = family members only | "Older" = general comparison
  • "Between" = 2 things | "Among" = 3+ things
  • Adjective + -er + than (not then): She is taller than him (not "then")
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Superlative → "the" + Adjective + est
  • More than 2 syllables → more/most
  • Elder = family only | Older = general
  • Good→Better→Best | Bad→Worse→Worst
  • Between (2) vs Among (3+)

⚡ Top 20 PNST Grammar Rules
  • 1. He/She/It → Simple Present → always add -s/-es
  • 2. Did/Does/Do + V₁ (base form always) → "He did not went" ❌ → "He did not go" ✅
  • 3. An = vowel SOUND (an hour ✅, an honest ✅, a university ✅)
  • 4. Since = point | For = duration (with perfect tenses)
  • 5. Stative verbs: No -ing form (know, love, want, see, understand)
  • 6. "A number of" → Plural | "The number of" → Singular
  • 7. Each/Every/Either/Neither → always Singular verb
  • 8. More + Comparative ❌ (more taller, most tallest)
  • 9. Passive: Object Pronoun → he→him, she→her, they→them, I→me, we→us
  • 10. Universal truth: No backshift in indirect speech
  • 11. Elder = family | Older = general | Between=2 | Among=3+
  • 12. Discuss, mention, describe, reach → No preposition after them
  • 13. Married to (not with) | Die of (disease) | Good at (not in)
  • 14. Its = possession | It's = it is | Their ≠ There ≠ They're
  • 15. Redundancy: Return back ❌, Repeat again ❌, Cousin brother ❌
  • 16. Good→Better→Best | Bad→Worse→Worst | Little→Less→Least
  • 17. Conditional: If + Simple Past → would + V₁
  • 18. Imperative Passive: Let + Object + be + V₃
  • 19. Intransitive verbs → No passive voice
  • 20. Simile = like/as | Metaphor = direct (no like/as)

Affect vs Effect ★ PNST
Affect = Verb (to affect = प्रभावित करना)। Effect = Noun (result = परिणाम)। Smoking affects health. The effect is harmful।
Stationary vs Stationery ★ PNST
Stationary (adj) = not moving (रुका हुआ)। Stationery (n) = pens, papers (लेखन सामग्री)।
Principle vs Principal ★ PNST
Principle = rule/belief (सिद्धांत)। Principal = head of school / main (प्रधान)।
Then vs Than
Than = comparison में (She is taller than me)। Then = time/sequence (First this, then that)।
