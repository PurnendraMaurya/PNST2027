भारतीय इतिहास
Ancient | Medieval | Modern | Freedom Struggle | Important Dates
▼
भारतीय इतिहास को तीन भागों में बांटते हैं: प्राचीन (Ancient), मध्यकालीन (Medieval), और आधुनिक (Modern)। PNST में Modern India और Freedom Struggle सबसे ज़्यादा पूछे जाते हैं।
🏺 प्राचीन भारत | Ancient India
  • Indus Valley Civilization (2500 BCE): Harappa, Mohenjo-daro। Town planning, drainage system। Decline: ~1800 BCE
  • Vedic Period: Rig Veda — oldest text। 4 Vedas: Rig, Yajur, Sama, Atharva
  • Maurya Empire: Chandragupta Maurya (321 BCE) — founder। Ashoka (273-232 BCE) — spread Buddhism after Kalinga war
  • Gupta Empire (320-550 CE): "Golden Age of India"। Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, Chandragupta II। Science, art, literature flourished
  • Harshavardhana (606-647 CE): Last great Hindu emperor। Chinese traveller Xuanzang visited
🕌 मध्यकालीन भारत | Medieval India
  • Delhi Sultanate (1206-1526): 5 dynasties — Slave, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, Lodi। Qutub Minar = Qutub-ud-Din Aibak
  • Alauddin Khilji: Market reforms। Mongol invasions repelled
  • Muhammad Bin Tughlaq: Shifted capital Delhi → Daulatabad। Token currency
  • Mughal Empire (1526-1857): Babur (founded, Battle of Panipat 1526) → Humayun → Akbar (1556-1605) → Jahangir → Shah Jahan (Taj Mahal) → Aurangzeb
  • Akbar: Din-i-Ilahi, Navratna, Abolish Jizya। Greatest Mughal emperor
  • Maratha Empire: Shivaji (1674) — Raigarh capital। Guerrilla warfare
  • Vijayanagara Empire: Krishnadeva Raya — greatest ruler। South India

भारतीय भूगोल
Rivers | Mountains | States | Climate | Soils | Natural Resources
▼
भारत — 7th largest country (3.28 million km²)। 29 States + 8 UTs। Population: ~140 crore (2nd largest)। Himalayas — N border, Indian Ocean — S।
⛰️ भौतिक विभाग | Physical Features
  • Himalayas: Youngest fold mountains। Highest peak in India = K2 (Pakistan-India border) / Kangchenjunga (India, 8586m)
  • Indo-Gangetic Plain: Most fertile plain। World's largest alluvial plain
  • Deccan Plateau: Triangular basalt plateau। Oldest landmass
  • Thar Desert (Rajasthan): Largest desert in India। Hot desert
  • Coastal Plains: Western (narrow, steep) + Eastern (wider, fertile)
  • Islands: Andaman & Nicobar (Bay of Bengal) + Lakshadweep (Arabian Sea)

🗺️ Important Facts
  • Largest State (area): Rajasthan | Smallest State: Goa
  • Most populous state: Uttar Pradesh | Least populous: Sikkim
  • Longest river: Ganga (2525 km) | Longest coastline state: Gujarat
  • Highest peak: Kangchenjunga (8586m) — in India. K2 (8611m) — disputed

  • Western Ghats: Biodiversity hotspot। Highest = Anai Mudi (2695m, Kerala)
🌦️ जलवायु | Climate of India
  • India = Tropical monsoon climate
  • SW Monsoon (June-Sept): Brings 75-80% of India's rainfall
  • Mawsynram, Meghalaya: Highest rainfall in world (~11,870 mm/year)
  • Leh, Ladakh: Least rainfall — cold desert
  • Chennai: NE monsoon from Bay of Bengal (Oct-Dec)

भारतीय राज्यव्यवस्था एवं संविधान
Constitution | Fundamental Rights | Parliament | President | DPSP
▼
भारतीय संविधान — विश्व का सबसे लंबा लिखित संविधान। 26 Nov 1949 को adopted, 26 Jan 1950 को लागू। B.R. Ambedkar = Father of Constitution।
📜 संविधान के मुख्य तथ्य | Constitution Facts
  • Adopted: 26 November 1949 (Constitution Day)
  • Enforced: 26 January 1950 (Republic Day)
  • Originally: 395 Articles, 8 Schedules, 22 Parts
  • Currently: 448 Articles, 12 Schedules, 25 Parts
  • Amendments: 106 Amendments till 2023
  • Preamble: Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic
  • Sources: UK (Parliamentary system), USA (Fundamental Rights, Judicial Review), Ireland (DPSP), Canada (Federal structure), Australia (Concurrent list)
🛡️ मूल अधिकार | Fundamental Rights (Part III)
RightArticlesContent
Right to Equality14-18समानता का अधिकार, caste/religion/gender discrimination ban
Right to Freedom19-22Speech, expression, movement, profession
Right against Exploitation23-24Forced labour & child labour ban
Right to Religion25-28Religious freedom
Cultural & Educational Rights29-30Minority rights
Right to Constitutional Remedies32Dr. Ambedkar: "Heart and Soul of Constitution"
⚖️ महत्वपूर्ण Articles | Important Articles
  • Article 1: Name and territory of India
  • Article 14: Equality before law
  • Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination
  • Article 19: Freedom of speech and expression
  • Article 21: Right to life and personal liberty (most important!)
  • Article 32: Right to move Supreme Court (constitutional remedy)
  • Article 44: Uniform Civil Code (DPSP)
  • Article 51A: Fundamental Duties (10+1 duties — 42nd + 86th amendment)
  • Article 112: Annual Financial Statement (Budget)
  • Article 280: Finance Commission
  • Article 324: Election Commission of India
  • Article 352: National Emergency
  • Article 356: President's Rule in State
  • Article 360: Financial Emergency
  • Article 370: Special status to J&K (abrogated 2019)
🏛️ संसद | Parliament Structure
  • Lok Sabha: Lower House। Max 552 members। Speaker = presiding officer। Tenure = 5 years
  • Rajya Sabha: Upper House। Max 250 members। VP = Chairman। Permanent house (never dissolved)
  • President: Constitutional head। Elected by Electoral College। 5-year term। Impeachment by Parliament
  • Prime Minister: Real executive head। Leader of Lok Sabha majority party। Art. 75
  • Supreme Court: Art. 124। Chief Justice + max 33 judges। Final court of appeal
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Constitution adopted = 26 Nov 1949 | Enforced = 26 Jan 1950
  • Ambedkar = Father of Constitution
  • Article 21 = Right to Life (most interpreted article)
  • Article 32 = "Heart and Soul" = Ambedkar
  • Preamble = Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic
  • Fundamental Rights = Part III (Articles 12-35)
  • DPSP = Part IV (Articles 36-51) — Non-justiciable

Article 21 ★ PNST
Right to Life and Personal Liberty। सबसे ज़्यादा interpret हुआ article। Privacy, Education, Health, Livelihood सब इसमें include।
Preamble ★ PNST
Constitution की "Introduction"। WE THE PEOPLE of India। Sovereign + Socialist + Secular + Democratic + Republic। 42nd Amendment (1976) ने Socialist और Secular add किए।
DPSP vs Fundamental Rights
Fundamental Rights = Justiciable (Court में enforce हो सकते हैं)। DPSP = Non-justiciable (moral obligations)।
Writ Petitions ★ PNST
Habeas Corpus (illegal detention), Mandamus (duty), Prohibition (inferior court), Certiorari (superior court), Quo Warranto (office validity)।

भारतीय अर्थव्यवस्था
GDP | Budget | RBI | Banks | Five Year Plans | Government Schemes
▼
India = 5th largest economy (GDP $3.7 trillion, 2024)। Mixed economy — Private + Public sector। Agriculture, Industry, Services — three sectors।
🏦 RBI — Reserve Bank of India
  • Founded: 1 April 1935। Nationalized: 1949
  • HQ: Mumbai। Governor = appointed by central govt
  • Functions: Currency issue, Monetary policy, Banker's bank, Foreign exchange
  • Repo Rate: Rate at which RBI lends to banks
  • Reverse Repo Rate: Rate at which banks deposit with RBI
  • CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio): % of deposits banks must keep with RBI
  • SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio): % banks must maintain in liquid assets
  • NITI Aayog: Replaced Planning Commission (2015)। Policy think tank

📊 Key Economic Terms

  • GNP: GDP + income from abroad - income paid abroad
  • Inflation: Increase in price level. CPI = Consumer Price Index
  • Union Budget: February 1 every year. Finance Minister presents it
