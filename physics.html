01
विद्युत आवेश तथा क्षेत्र
Coulomb's Law | Electric Field | Gauss's Law | Dipole
▼
Electric charge — fundamental property of matter। Benjamin Franklin ने positive/negative charge की concept दी। Charge quantized होता है: q = ne (n = integer, e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C)।
⚡ Electric Charge के गुण
  • Quantization: q = ne। e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C। Charge smallest unit = charge of electron
  • Conservation: Charge create/destroy नहीं होता — isolated system में total charge constant
  • Additivity: q_total = q₁ + q₂ + q₃ ... (algebraic sum)
⚡ Coulomb's Law
Coulomb's Law:
F = k·q₁q₂/r² = (1/4πε₀)·q₁q₂/r² k = 9×10⁹ N m² C⁻², ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² C² N⁻¹ m⁻²
  • Force along line joining two charges। Like charges repel, Unlike attract
  • Principle of superposition: Force on q due to multiple charges = vector sum
  • In medium: F_medium = F_vacuum / εr (εr = relative permittivity/dielectric constant)
🌀 Electric Field (E)
  • E = F/q₀ (force per unit test charge)
  • Point charge: E = kq/r² (radially outward for +ve, inward for -ve)
  • Electric field lines: Start from +ve, end at -ve। Never intersect। Density ∝ field strength
  • Uniform field: Parallel equidistant lines (e.g., between parallel plates)
🔵 Electric Dipole
  • Two equal & opposite charges ±q separated by 2a
  • Dipole moment: p = q × 2a (from -ve to +ve, direction)
  • Field on axial point: E = 2kp/r³ | Field on equatorial point: E = kp/r³
  • Torque on dipole: τ = p × E = pE sinθ
📐 Gauss's Law
Gauss's Law:
φ = ∮E·dA = q_enclosed / ε₀
  • Total electric flux through closed surface = q_enclosed / ε₀
  • Infinite line charge: E = λ/2πε₀r
  • Infinite plane sheet: E = σ/2ε₀ (each side)
  • Conducting sphere: E = 0 inside, E = q/4πε₀r² outside
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C | k = 9×10⁹ N m² C⁻²
  • Coulomb's law: F ∝ q₁q₂, F ∝ 1/r²
  • Gauss's law: φ = q/ε₀
  • E inside conductor = 0
  • Electric field lines never intersect
  • Dipole moment p = q×2a (−ve to +ve direction)

02
स्थिरवैद्युत विभव तथा धारिता
Potential | Capacitor | Energy | Dielectric | Series Parallel
▼
Electric potential = work done per unit charge to bring test charge from infinity। V = W/q₀। Scalar quantity। Unit = Volt (J/C)।
📊 Electric Potential
  • V = kq/r (point charge)। V ∝ 1/r (unlike E ∝ 1/r²)
  • Equipotential surfaces: Same potential → no work done moving along it। ⊥ to field lines
  • E = −dV/dr (E = negative gradient of V)
  • Potential due to dipole: V_axial = kp/r², V_equatorial = 0
  • Conductor surface = equipotential। E = 0 inside conductor
🔋 Capacitor & Capacitance
  • C = Q/V (unit = Farad, F)
  • Parallel plate capacitor: C = ε₀A/d (A=plate area, d=separation)
  • With dielectric: C = Kε₀A/d (K = dielectric constant)
  • Series: 1/C = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + 1/C₃ (same charge, different voltage)
  • Parallel: C = C₁ + C₂ + C₃ (same voltage, different charge)
  • Energy stored: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C = QV/2
  • Energy density: u = ½ε₀E²
🔬 Dielectrics
  • Polar dielectrics: permanent dipole (H₂O, HCl)
  • Non-polar: induced dipole in E field (O₂, H₂)
  • Dielectric slab inserted → C increases (K times)
  • If battery connected: V same, Q increases, E same
  • If battery disconnected: Q same, V decreases, E decreases
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • C = ε₀A/d | With dielectric: C = Kε₀A/d
  • Energy = ½CV² = Q²/2C
  • Series capacitors: 1/C_total = 1/C₁+1/C₂
  • Parallel capacitors: C_total = C₁+C₂
  • Equipotential ⊥ field lines
  • E = -dV/dr

03
विद्युत धारा
Ohm's Law | Resistance | Kirchhoff | Wheatstone | Potentiometer
▼
Electric current = Rate of flow of charge। I = dQ/dt। Unit = Ampere (A)। Conventional current = +ve charge flow direction।
⚡ Ohm's Law & Resistance
  • Ohm's Law: V = IR (V=voltage, I=current, R=resistance)
  • R = ρL/A (ρ=resistivity, L=length, A=cross-section area)
  • R increases with temperature: ρ = ρ₀(1+αT) where α = temperature coefficient
  • Series: R = R₁+R₂+R₃ (same current, different voltage)
  • Parallel: 1/R = 1/R₁+1/R₂+1/R₃ (same voltage, different current)
  • Drift velocity (vd): I = neAvd। Electrons slowly drift in conductor
  • Mobility (μ) = vd/E = eτ/m (τ = relaxation time)
🔋 EMF & Internal Resistance
  • EMF (ε) = work done per unit charge by source
  • Terminal voltage: V = ε − Ir (during discharge) | V = ε + Ir (during charging)
  • Cells in series: ε_total = ε₁+ε₂, r_total = r₁+r₂
  • Cells in parallel: ε_eq = (ε₁r₂+ε₂r₁)/(r₁+r₂)
📐 Kirchhoff's Laws
  • KCL (Junction rule): ΣI = 0 at junction (charge conservation)
  • KVL (Loop rule): ΣV = 0 in closed loop (energy conservation)
  • Wheatstone bridge: P/Q = R/S (balanced condition — galvanometer shows zero)
  • Meter bridge: Uses Wheatstone principle। R/S = l/(100-l)
📏 Potentiometer
  • Uniform wire — potential drops uniformly along length
  • Compare EMFs: ε₁/ε₂ = l₁/l₂
  • Find internal resistance: r = R(l₁-l₂)/l₂
  • Advantage over voltmeter: No current drawn from circuit (ideal voltmeter)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • V = IR | R = ρL/A
  • Series R: add | Parallel R: reciprocal add
  • Wheatstone: P/Q = R/S (balanced)
  • KCL = charge conservation | KVL = energy conservation
  • Terminal V = ε - Ir (discharge)
  • Potentiometer: no current drawn = more accurate

04
गतिमान आवेश और चुम्बकत्व
Biot-Savart | Ampere | Lorentz Force | Galvanometer | Cyclotron
▼
Moving charges create magnetic field। Hans Christian Oersted (1820) ने पहली बार current-carrying wire से magnetic field देखा।
🧲 Biot-Savart Law
Biot-Savart Law:
dB = (μ₀/4π) × (Idl sinθ)/r² μ₀ = 4π×10⁻⁷ T m A⁻¹
  • Circular loop center: B = μ₀I/2R
  • Straight infinite wire: B = μ₀I/2πr
  • Direction: Right-hand thumb rule (thumb = current, fingers curl = B)
📐 Ampere's Circuital Law
∮B·dl = μ₀I_enclosed
  • Solenoid: B = μ₀nI (n = turns per unit length). Inside uniform, outside ≈ 0
  • Toroid: B = μ₀nI (inside), B = 0 (outside)
⚡ Lorentz Force
F = q(E + v×B) = qvB sinθ (magnetic part)
  • Direction: Fleming's Left-hand rule (FBI rule)
  • Magnetic force does NO work (⊥ to velocity)
  • Circular motion in uniform B: r = mv/qB, T = 2πm/qB
  • Cyclotron: Accelerates charged particles। Cyclotron frequency: ν = qB/2πm (mass independent)
🔬 Galvanometer & Instruments
  • Current sensitivity = Nba/k (N=turns, b=breadth, a=length, k=torsion constant)
  • Ammeter: Galvanometer + Low resistance shunt (parallel)
  • Voltmeter: Galvanometer + High resistance (series)
  • Force between parallel wires: F/L = μ₀I₁I₂/2πd
  • Same direction currents → Attractive | Opposite → Repulsive
  • Definition of Ampere: 1A produces force of 2×10⁻⁷ N/m between wires 1m apart
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Solenoid B = μ₀nI | Circular loop B = μ₀I/2R
  • Lorentz force = qvB sinθ (no work done)
  • Cyclotron frequency = qB/2πm (mass independent)
  • Ammeter: shunt parallel | Voltmeter: resistance series
  • Parallel wires same current = attractive force

05
चुम्बकत्व एवं द्रव्य
Bar Magnet | Earth's Magnetism | Dia/Para/Ferro | Curie Law
▼
Bar magnet एक magnetic dipole है। Isolated magnetic poles exist नहीं करते। Every magnet has N and S poles together।
🧲 Bar Magnet
  • Magnetic dipole moment: m = M (unit: A m²)
  • Axial field: B = μ₀/4π × 2M/r³
  • Equatorial field: B = μ₀/4π × M/r³ (direction opposite to M)
  • Torque: τ = MB sinθ | PE: U = -MB cosθ
  • Time period of oscillation: T = 2π√(I/MB)
🌍 Earth's Magnetism
  • Geographic meridian: N-S geographic poles वाला plane
  • Magnetic meridian: Magnetic N-S वाला plane
  • Declination (α): Angle between magnetic & geographic meridians
  • Dip/Inclination (δ): Angle between total B and horizontal plane
  • At magnetic equator: dip = 0°। At poles: dip = 90°
  • BH = B cosδ (horizontal component), BV = B sinδ (vertical)
🔬 Magnetic Properties of Materials
  • Diamagnetic: Weakly repelled by B। χm = small negative। e.g., Bi, Cu, Water, NaCl
  • Paramagnetic: Weakly attracted। χm = small positive। e.g., Al, Mn, Pt, O₂
  • Ferromagnetic: Strongly attracted। χm = large positive। e.g., Fe, Ni, Co
  • Curie's Law: χm = C/T (paramagnetic materials, C=Curie constant)
  • Curie temperature: Above this, ferromagnetic → paramagnetic
  • Hysteresis: Lag of B behind H। Soft iron = small loop (transformer core). Steel = large loop (permanent magnet)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Diamagnetic: repelled, χm < 0 (Bismuth best)
  • Paramagnetic: attracted, χm > 0 small
  • Ferromagnetic: strongly attracted (Fe, Ni, Co)
  • Curie temperature: Fe=1043K, Ni=631K, Co=1388K
  • Magnetic equator: dip=0° | Poles: dip=90°

वैद्युतचुम्बकीय प्रेरण
Faraday's Law | Lenz's Law | Motional EMF | Self/Mutual Inductance
▼
Michael Faraday (1831) — Changing magnetic flux induces EMF। यह electromagnetic induction की खोज थी।
⚡ Faraday's Laws
  • First law: जब magnetic flux change होता है, EMF induce होती है
  • Second law: ε = -dΦ/dt (induced EMF = rate of change of flux)
  • Magnetic flux: Φ = BA cosθ (unit = Weber = T m²)
  • Lenz's Law: Induced current की direction such that it opposes the cause (energy conservation)
🔌 Motional EMF
  • Conductor moving in magnetic field: ε = Bvl sinθ
  • ε = Blv (when rod ⊥ to B and v)
  • Eddy currents: Induced in solid conductors। Cause heating (useful: induction cooker, brakes)
  • Minimize eddy currents: Laminations (thin sheets) in transformer cores
🔄 Inductance
  • Self inductance (L): Φ = LI → ε = -L(dI/dt)
  • Solenoid: L = μ₀n²Al (n=turns/length, A=area, l=length)
  • Mutual inductance (M): Flux in 2 due to current in 1 → ε₂ = -M(dI₁/dt)
  • M depends on geometry, orientation, medium between coils
  • L series: L = L₁+L₂ (no coupling) | L parallel: 1/L = 1/L₁+1/L₂
  • Energy stored in inductor: U = ½LI²
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Faraday: ε = -dΦ/dt
  • Lenz's law = energy conservation (opposes change)
  • Motional EMF = Blv
  • Self inductance energy = ½LI²
  • Eddy currents = minimize with laminations
  • Solenoid: L = μ₀n²Al

प्रत्यावर्ती धारा
RMS | Impedance | Resonance | Power Factor | Transformer
▼
AC voltage: v = V₀ sinωt। India में: 220V (rms), 50 Hz। AC generators और transformers इसी principle पर।
📊 AC Circuits
  • Resistor (R): V and I in phase। V_R = IR
  • Inductor (L): Current lags voltage by 90°। X_L = ωL (inductive reactance)
  • Capacitor (C): Current leads voltage by 90°। X_C = 1/ωC (capacitive reactance)
  • RMS values: I_rms = I₀/√2 = 0.707 I₀ | V_rms = V₀/√2
🔌 Series LCR Circuit
Impedance & Resonance:
Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²) Resonance: ω₀ = 1/√LC → Z = R (minimum)
  • Resonance condition: X_L = X_C → ω₀ = 1/√(LC). Current maximum
  • Q-factor (sharpness): Q = ω₀L/R = 1/ω₀CR. Higher Q = sharper resonance
  • Resonance frequency: f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)
⚡ Power in AC Circuits
  • P = V_rms I_rms cosφ (cosφ = power factor)
  • Pure R: φ=0, P=V_rms × I_rms (maximum)
  • Pure L or C: φ=90°, P=0 (wattless current)
  • Wattless current: Component that does no work (90° out of phase)
🔄 Transformer
  • V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p = I_p/I_s
  • Step-up: N_s > N_p (voltage increases, current decreases)
  • Step-down: N_s < N_p (voltage decreases, current increases)
  • Ideal transformer: V_p I_p = V_s I_s (100% efficiency)
  • Losses: Copper loss (I²R), Iron/Core loss (eddy currents + hysteresis)
  • Power transmission: High voltage → low current → low I²R loss
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • I_rms = I₀/√2 | V_rms = V₀/√2
  • Resonance: X_L=X_C, ω₀=1/√LC, Z=R
  • Power factor = cosφ | Pure L,C → P=0 (wattless)
  • Transformer: Vs/Vp = Ns/Np
  • High voltage transmission → less I²R loss

वैद्युतचुम्बकीय तरंगें
Maxwell | EM Spectrum | Speed of Light | Properties
▼
Maxwell ने predict किया कि changing electric field creates magnetic field (Displacement current)। EM waves = self-propagating E and B fields। c = 3×10⁸ m/s।
🌊 EM Waves की विशेषताएं
  • Transverse waves। E and B ⊥ to each other AND ⊥ to direction of propagation
  • Speed in vacuum: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3×10⁸ m/s
  • No medium required। Travel through vacuum
  • E₀/B₀ = c (ratio of amplitudes)
  • Displacement current: I_d = ε₀ dΦ_E/dt (Maxwell's contribution)
🌈 EM Spectrum (increasing wavelength)
  • Gamma rays (λ < 10⁻¹²m) — Nuclear reactions, Cancer treatment
  • X-rays (10⁻¹² to 10⁻⁸m) — Medical imaging, Crystallography
  • Ultraviolet (10⁻⁸ to 4×10⁻⁷m) — Sterilization, Sun
  • Visible light (4×10⁻⁷ to 7×10⁻⁷m) — VIBGYOR (Violet 400nm - Red 700nm)
  • Infrared (7×10⁻⁷ to 10⁻³m) — Heat, Night vision, Cooking
  • Microwaves (10⁻³ to 0.1m) — Microwave oven, RADAR
  • Radio waves (0.1m to 10⁴m) — Radio, TV broadcast
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • c = 3×10⁸ m/s (speed of light in vacuum)
  • EM waves: Transverse, E⊥B⊥direction
  • Gamma rays: highest frequency, shortest λ
  • Radio waves: lowest frequency, longest λ
  • UV rays: ozone layer absorbs them
  • Maxwell = Displacement current → EM wave theory

▼
Ray optics: Light travels in straight lines (rays)। Laws of reflection and refraction। Snell's law: n₁ sinθ₁ = n₂ sinθ₂।
🪞 Mirrors
  • Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f | f = R/2
  • Magnification: m = -v/u
  • Sign convention: incident ray direction = +ve
  • Concave: Real inverted image (beyond F) | Virtual erect (inside F)
  • Convex: Always virtual, erect, diminished image
🔬 Refraction & TIR
  • Snell's Law: n₁ sinθ₁ = n₂ sinθ₂ | n = c/v
  • Total Internal Reflection (TIR): θ > critical angle, denser to rarer medium
  • sinC = n₂/n₁ = 1/n (if n₂ = air)
  • Applications: Optical fibre, Mirages, Diamonds
  • Refraction at spherical surface: n₁/u + n₂/v = (n₂-n₁)/R
🔍 Lenses
  • Lens formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f
  • Power: P = 1/f (unit = Dioptre, D)
  • Lensmaker's equation: 1/f = (n-1)(1/R₁ - 1/R₂)
  • Combined lenses: 1/f = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂ | P = P₁+P₂
  • Convex lens: Converging (+ve f) | Concave: Diverging (-ve f)
🌈 Prism & Dispersion
  • δ = (n-1)A (thin prism, minimum deviation)
  • At minimum deviation: r = A/2, i = (A+δm)/2
  • n = sin[(A+δm)/2] / sin(A/2)
  • Dispersion: Violet bends more, Red bends less (VIBGYOR)
  • Rainbow: Primary (red outside, violet inside) | Secondary (reversed)
🔭 Optical Instruments
  • Simple microscope: m = 1 + D/f (D=25cm, least distance of distinct vision)
  • Compound microscope: m = m₀ × me = (L/f₀)(1+D/fe)
  • Telescope (refracting): m = f₀/fe (angular magnification)
  • Reflecting telescope: Large aperture → high resolution। No chromatic aberration
  • Resolving power: RP = 2n sinθ/λ (microscope) | RP = D/1.22λ (telescope)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • 1/v + 1/u = 1/f (mirrors) | 1/v - 1/u = 1/f (lens)
  • TIR: θ > critical angle, dense to rare medium
  • P = 1/f (Dioptre) | Combined: P = P₁+P₂
  • Human eye: Near point=25cm, Far point=∞
  • Myopia: concave lens | Hypermetropia: convex lens

तरंग प्रकाशिकी
Huygens | Interference | Young's Experiment | Diffraction | Polarisation
▼
Wave optics: Light as a wave। Huygens' principle, Interference, Diffraction, Polarisation — wave nature prove करते हैं।
🌊 Huygens' Principle
  • Every point on wavefront = new source of secondary wavelets
  • Explains reflection, refraction, diffraction
  • Wavefront: Surface where all points have same phase
🌈 Young's Double Slit Experiment (YDSE)
  • Conditions: Coherent sources, same frequency
  • Path difference: Δx = d sinθ ≈ yd/D
  • Bright fringes (maxima): Δx = nλ
  • Dark fringes (minima): Δx = (2n-1)λ/2
  • Fringe width: β = λD/d (D=screen distance, d=slit separation)
  • In medium (μ): β' = β/μ (fringe width decreases)
  • I = I₀ cos²(δ/2) where δ = phase difference
🔬 Diffraction
  • Bending of light around obstacles (single slit)
  • Central maximum: widest and brightest
  • Minima: a sinθ = nλ (a = slit width)
  • Central fringe width = 2λD/a (twice other fringes)
  • Resolving power: limit of resolution = 1.22 λ/D (telescope)
🕶️ Polarisation
  • Only transverse waves polarise (proof that light is transverse)
  • Malus's Law: I = I₀ cos²θ (polarizer + analyser at angle θ)
  • Polarisation by reflection: Brewster's law: tan i_p = n (i_p = Brewster angle)
  • At Brewster angle: reflected + refracted rays ⊥ to each other
  • Uses: Sunglasses, Photography, LCD screens
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Fringe width β = λD/d
  • Bright fringes: Δx = nλ | Dark: Δx = (2n-1)λ/2
  • Malus's Law: I = I₀ cos²θ
  • Brewster's Law: tan i_p = n
  • Diffraction proves wave nature of light

विकिरण तथा द्रव्य की द्वैत प्रकृति
Photoelectric Effect | Einstein | de Broglie | Davisson-Germer
▼
Wave-Particle duality: Light behaves as wave (interference, diffraction) AND as particle (photoelectric effect)। Matter also shows wave nature (de Broglie)।
💡 Photoelectric Effect
  • Heinrich Hertz (1887) — observed। J.J. Thomson — electrons emitted
  • Einstein's explanation (1905): Light = photons (quanta of energy)
  • E = hν = hc/λ। h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s (Planck's constant)
  • Threshold frequency (ν₀): Minimum frequency for emission. φ = hν₀ (work function)
  • Einstein's equation: hν = φ + ½mv²_max = hν₀ + eV₀
  • Stopping potential V₀: ½mv²_max = eV₀
  • No emission below threshold (intensity doesn't matter!)
  • Instantaneous emission (no time lag)
  • KE_max ∝ frequency (not intensity)
  • Photoelectric current ∝ intensity
🌊 de Broglie Hypothesis
de Broglie wavelength:
λ = h/p = h/mv = h/√(2mKE) = h/√(2meV)
  • Matter has wave nature! Particles have wavelength
  • Electron: λ = 1.227/√V nm (V = accelerating voltage)
  • Davisson-Germer experiment (1927): Electron diffraction → proved wave nature
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • E = hν = hc/λ | h = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ J·s
  • KE_max = hν - φ (Einstein's photoelectric eq.)
  • Threshold frequency: emission stop below it
  • de Broglie: λ = h/mv
  • Davisson-Germer = electron wave nature proved

▼
Thomson → Plum pudding model। Rutherford (1911) → Nuclear model। Bohr (1913) → Quantized orbits for Hydrogen atom।
🔬 Atomic Models
  • Thomson model: Positive sphere + electrons embedded। "Plum pudding"
  • Rutherford's experiment: α-particles on gold foil. Most pass through, few deflect, rare bounce back
  • Conclusion: Small dense positive nucleus। Electrons in orbits। Atom mostly empty
  • Limitation: Accelerating electrons → should radiate → atom should collapse (doesn't explain stability)
⚛️ Bohr's Model (Hydrogen)
  • Postulates: Electrons in specific orbits (stationary states). No radiation in orbit. Radiation during transition
  • Angular momentum quantization: mvr = nh/2π = nħ
  • Radius: r_n = n²a₀ (a₀ = 0.529 Å = Bohr radius)
  • Energy: E_n = -13.6/n² eV
  • E₁ = -13.6 eV (ground state), E₂ = -3.4 eV, E₃ = -1.51 eV
  • Ionization energy = 13.6 eV (from ground state)
🌈 Spectral Series of Hydrogen
  • Lyman series: n₂ → n₁=1 (UV region)
  • Balmer series: n₂ → n₁=2 (Visible region) — H_α (red), H_β, H_γ
  • Paschen series: n₂ → n₁=3 (IR region)
  • Brackett series: n₂ → n₁=4 (Far IR)
  • Pfund series: n₂ → n₁=5 (Far IR)
  • Rydberg formula: 1/λ = R_H(1/n₁² - 1/n₂²) [R_H = 1.097×10⁷ m⁻¹]
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • E_n = -13.6/n² eV | r_n = n²×0.529 Å
  • Ground state: n=1, E=-13.6 eV
  • Lyman=UV, Balmer=Visible, Paschen=IR
  • Ionization energy = 13.6 eV
  • Rutherford: most α-pass through (atom mostly empty)

नाभिक
Binding Energy | Radioactivity | Nuclear Fission | Fusion
▼
Nucleus = Protons + Neutrons (Nucleons)। Nuclear radius: R = R₀A^(1/3) [R₀ = 1.2 fm]। Nuclear density = constant (independent of A)।
⚛️ Nuclear Properties
  • Atomic number (Z): Proton number। Mass number (A): Proton + Neutron (A=Z+N)
  • Isotopes: Same Z, different A (e.g., H-1, H-2, H-3)
  • Isobars: Same A, different Z (e.g., C-14, N-14)
  • Isotones: Same N, different Z
  • Nuclear density ≈ 2.3×10¹⁷ kg/m³ (same for all nuclei)
💥 Binding Energy
  • Mass defect: Δm = Zmp + Nmn - M_nucleus
  • Binding energy: BE = Δm × c² (1 amu = 931.5 MeV)
  • BE/nucleon: Maximum ≈ 8.8 MeV for Fe-56 (most stable)
  • Fission: Heavy → medium (BE/nucleon increases → energy released)
  • Fusion: Light → medium (BE/nucleon increases → energy released)
☢️ Radioactivity
  • Henri Becquerel (1896) discovered। Spontaneous emission
  • α decay: ₂⁴He emitted → Z-2, A-4। Range: few cm in air
  • β decay: Electron/positron emitted → Z±1, A same। n→p+e⁻+ν̄
  • γ decay: High energy photon → Z,A same। No change in Z or A
  • Radioactive decay law: N = N₀e^(-λt)
  • Half-life: T₁/₂ = 0.693/λ (First order = same as chemical kinetics!)
  • Mean life: τ = 1/λ = T₁/₂/0.693
⚡ Nuclear Reactions
  • Fission: ²³⁵U + n → ⁹²Kr + ¹⁴¹Ba + 3n + energy (chain reaction)
  • Critical mass required for sustained chain reaction
  • Fusion: ²H + ²H → ³He + n + energy (requires very high temperature)
  • Sun's energy = Fusion। Hydrogen bomb = Uncontrolled fusion
  • Nuclear reactor = Controlled fission
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • T₁/₂ = 0.693/λ | N = N₀e^(-λt)
  • Fe-56 = most stable (max BE/nucleon)
  • α: Z-2,A-4 | β: Z±1,A same | γ: Z,A same
  • Fission: U-235 + n → chain reaction
  • 1 amu = 931.5 MeV (mass-energy)

अर्धचालक इलेक्ट्रॉनिकी
p-n Junction | Diode | Transistor | Logic Gates | LED
▼
Semiconductors: Eg ~ 1 eV (Si=1.1, Ge=0.7 eV)। Conductors: Eg=0। Insulators: Eg>3 eV। Transistor discovered 1948 (Shockley, Bardeen, Brattain) — Nobel 1956।
🔬 Types of Semiconductors
  • Intrinsic: Pure semiconductor। n_e = n_h। Si, Ge
  • n-type: Pentavalent dopant (P, As, Sb). Electrons = majority carriers
  • p-type: Trivalent dopant (B, Al, In). Holes = majority carriers
  • Conductivity: σ = e(n_e μ_e + n_h μ_h)
  • n_e × n_h = n_i² (Law of mass action)
🔌 p-n Junction Diode
  • Depletion region: No free carriers at junction। Built-in potential barrier
  • Forward bias: P → +ve, N → -ve। Barrier decreases → Current flows
  • Reverse bias: P → -ve, N → +ve। Barrier increases → No current (small leakage)
  • Zener diode: Works in reverse breakdown। Voltage regulator
  • LED: Forward bias → recombination → light emission. GaAs, GaP
  • Photodiode: Reverse bias → light → current। Photodetector
  • Solar cell: p-n junction → photovoltaic effect → generates EMF
📡 Rectifier
  • Half-wave rectifier: One diode। Only positive half cycle passes. η=40.6%
  • Full-wave rectifier: 4 diodes (Bridge) or 2 diodes (centre-tap). Both half cycles. η=81.2%
  • Filter capacitor: Smooth DC output
🔧 Transistor
  • 3 layers: Emitter (E), Base (B), Collector (C)। NPN or PNP
  • Transistor as amplifier (CE config): Current gain β = I_C/I_B
  • β = 20-200 (typical). Voltage gain = β × R_C/R_in
  • Transistor as switch: Saturation (ON) | Cutoff (OFF)
  • I_E = I_C + I_B
🔢 Logic Gates
  • AND gate: Y = A·B (both inputs 1 → output 1)
  • OR gate: Y = A+B (any input 1 → output 1)
  • NOT gate: Y = Ā (inverts)
  • NAND gate: Y = ̄(A·B) = Universal gate
  • NOR gate: Y = ̄(A+B) = Universal gate
  • NAND and NOR = Universal gates (any gate can be made)
⭐ PNST Important Points
  • Si bandgap=1.1eV | Ge=0.7eV
  • n-type: Pentavalent | p-type: Trivalent dopant
  • Zener diode = Voltage regulator
  • LED: Forward bias, recombination → light
  • NAND/NOR = Universal gates
  • Transistor: I_E = I_B + I_C
